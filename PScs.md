## 1. Voice & Tone System

PeakSeen's voice is constant. Tone adapts to context.

### The Voice (Always)

| Trait | What It Sounds Like | What It Never Sounds Like |
|-------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **Precise** | Short sentences. Active verbs. No filler. | Rambling. Over-explained. Hedge words ("sort of", "kind of", "basically"). |
| **Confident** | Declarative. Assertive. No apologies. | Arrogant. Aggressive. Salesy exclamation marks. |
| **Intelligent** | Strategic framing. Industry-aware. References real problems. | Academic jargon. Overly technical without context. Condescending. |
| **Minimal** | One strong sentence over three weak ones. White space in writing. | Wall-of-text paragraphs. Redundant phrases. Saying the same thing twice. |
| **Warm (not corporate)** | "We" and "your". Direct address. Human inflection. | "Pursuant to", "synergy", "leverage", "utilize". Corporate emptiness. |

### Tone Spectrum by Context

| Context | Tone Dial | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Hero headlines** | Maximum confidence + minimum words | "Reach Your Peak. Be Seen." |
| **Service descriptions** | Strategic + clear | "A brand identity isn't a logo. It's the system that makes your business recognisable, trusted, and chosen." |
| **Tool landing pages** | Inviting + action-oriented | "Answer 10 questions. Get a score. Know exactly where your brand stands." |
| **Blog posts** | Insightful + conversational | "Most startups skip brand strategy because it feels expensive. It's not. Skipping it is." |
| **Contact / forms** | Warm + direct | "Tell us what you're building. We'll tell you how we can help." |
| **Error states** | Human + helpful | "Something went wrong on our end. Try refreshing, or email us at hello@peakseen.com." |
| **Empty states** | Honest + aspirational | "We're just getting started. These are concept projects — real client work is on the way." |
| **Social proof** | Factual + understated | "5 projects. 3 industries. 1 mission." (not "AMAZING results for HUNDREDS of clients!!!") |

### Words We Use

`define` · `build` · `design` · `launch` · `clarity` · `purpose` · `rise` · `peak` · `craft` · `strategy` · `transform` · `elevate` · `precision` · `intentional` · `visible`

### Words We Avoid

`leverage` · `synergy` · `utilize` · `revolutionary` · `game-changing` · `world-class` · `cutting-edge` · `best-in-class` · `skyrocket` · `crush it` · `hustle` · `guru` · `ninja`

## 2. Writing Rules

These rules apply to every piece of copy across the site — pages, buttons, tooltips, error messages, emails, and metadata.

### Sentence Structure

1. **Lead with the value, not the feature.** "Know where your brand stands" before "Take our 10-question quiz."
2. **One idea per sentence.** If a sentence has a comma followed by a new thought, split it.
3. **Front-load the important word.** "Brand identity design for startups" not "For startups looking for brand identity design."
4. **Active voice always.** "We design brands" not "Brands are designed by us."
5. **Vary sentence length.** Short sentence. Then a slightly longer one that expands the thought. Then short again.

### Headline Hierarchy per Page

Every page follows a strict content hierarchy:

| Level | Name | Purpose | Format |
|-------|------|---------|--------|
| **H1** | Page headline | One per page. Contains the primary keyword. Communicates the page's purpose in under 8 words. | Display font, hero size |
| **Subheadline** | Supporting line | Expands the H1 with a single sentence of context. 15–25 words max. | Body font, 20px |
| **H2** | Section headline | Introduces each major section on the page. | Display font, section size |
| **H3** | Subsection label | Introduces cards, tiers, or grouped items within a section. | Display font, card-title size |
| **Body** | Paragraph copy | Explains, persuades, or informs. Max 3–4 sentences per paragraph. | Body font, 16–18px |
| **CTA** | Action text | Tells the user exactly what happens next. Always starts with a verb. | Display font, button style |

### CTA Writing Rules

| Rule | Good | Bad |
|------|------|-----|
| Start with a verb | "Start a Project" | "Project Inquiry" |
| Be specific about the outcome | "Get My Free Brand Report" | "Submit" |
| Use "→" for forward motion | "View all services →" | "Click here" |
| Never use "Click here" or "Learn more" alone | "See the full case study →" | "Learn more" |
| Match the user's intent stage | "Book a Free Call" (high intent) vs "Try it free" (low intent) | Using "Buy Now" on a free tool |

### Microcopy Standards

| Element | Rule | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Form labels | Sentence case, no colons | `Your email` not `Email:` |
| Placeholder text | Show format, not instruction | `you@company.com` not `Enter your email address here` |
| Error messages | Say what went wrong + how to fix it | `Please enter a valid email address` not `Error` |
| Success messages | Confirm the action + say what happens next | `Brief sent! We'll respond within 24 hours.` |
| Tooltips | One sentence max, no period | `This is your overall brand health score` |
| Empty states | Acknowledge + redirect | `No case studies yet. We're working on our first client projects.` |
| Loading states | Never blank. Always a status message. | `Generating your brand names...` |
| Badge/label text | 1–2 words, no verbs | `Most Popular` · `New` · `Concept Work` |

### Our Story

**Structure:** Narrative prose — 3–4 paragraphs. Written in first person plural ("we") with the founder's voice present.

**Content brief:**

| Paragraph | Content |
|-----------|---------|
| **1 — The origin** | Why PeakSeen exists. What problem the founder kept seeing. The gap in the market — businesses with real value but invisible brands. A specific anecdote or observation that triggered the founding. |
| **2 — The mission** | What PeakSeen actually does differently. The "purpose-first" approach. Not just making things pretty — making them strategic. |
| **3 — Where we are now** | Current state — honest about being early-stage if true. "We're a small studio with big ambitions" is more credible than pretending to be a 50-person agency. |
| **4 — Where we're going** | The Venture-as-a-Service vision. Becoming the studio where African companies (and eventually global ones) come to define and launch the future of their brands. |

| **Introduction** | 2–3 paragraphs. Hook the reader with a relatable problem or bold statement. State what they'll learn. No "In this article, we will discuss..." preamble. |

### Blog Post Voice Rules

1. **Open with a problem, not a definition.** "Most startups skip brand strategy because it feels expensive." not "Brand strategy is the process of..."
2. **Use "you" more than "we".** The post is for the reader, not about PeakSeen.
3. **Include one bold, quotable line per post.** Something worth screenshotting and sharing. Wrap it in a callout.
4. **End with an action, not a summary.** "Your next step: download the Brand Clarity Workbook and complete Section 2" not "We hope this article was helpful."
5. **Never use "In conclusion" or "To summarise."** Just end.
