## Overview

A B2B SaaS startup building workforce management tools for SMEs across West Africa needed two things simultaneously: a brand identity that would earn the trust of enterprise buyers, and a product design system that their engineering team could build from consistently.

## The Challenge

The startup had a working product but no design foundation beneath it. UI components had been built ad hoc by different developers over 18 months, resulting in visual inconsistency that made the product feel unfinished — even when the underlying features were solid.

At the same time, their brand materials (website, pitch deck, sales one-pagers) used three different versions of their logo, four different primary colours, and no consistent typography.

Investors and enterprise buyers noticed. The brand was creating friction the product did not deserve.

## The Approach

We treated this as a brand and product design challenge simultaneously, understanding that the two had to be unified by a single visual language.

The brand strategy started with their core audience: operations managers and HR directors at SMEs who needed powerful tools without enterprise-level complexity. The brand had to communicate capability without intimidation.

We defined a visual direction built around confidence and clarity: a focused colour palette of deep navy and bright amber, geometric sans-serif typography, and a design language that prioritised information density over decorative elements.

## The Work

The engagement delivered two interconnected systems:

**Brand Identity:**
- Logo system with responsive variations
- Colour palette with semantic usage rules
- Typography system (display, body, data)
- Iconography style guide
- Brand voice and tone documentation

**Product Design System:**
- 47 reusable UI components built in Figma
- Design token library (colour, spacing, typography, radius, shadow)
- Component documentation with usage guidelines
- Dark mode token mapping
- Handoff documentation for the engineering team

## The Outcome

The engineering team adopted the design system immediately. Within two months, new feature UI development time had dropped by 40% because developers were composing from a documented component library rather than building from scratch. The rebrand also contributed to a successful Series A pitch — three investors specifically cited the quality of the brand and product experience as factors in their decision.
