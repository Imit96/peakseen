## Overview

A fast-growing e-commerce business selling handmade Nigerian fashion accessories needed a brand identity that communicated craftsmanship, cultural pride, and modern style — all at once. Their existing presence was a patchwork of mismatched visuals that failed to reflect the quality of the products they sold.

## The Challenge

The business had strong products and a loyal customer base but no cohesive brand to match. Their Instagram grid looked different every month, their website felt generic, and their packaging gave no indication of the thought and care that went into each piece.

The result was a disconnect: premium products, discount-brand perception.

## The Approach

We began with a discovery session to understand the founder's vision, the target customer, and the cultural story behind the brand. What emerged was a clear positioning: a modern celebration of Nigerian craft heritage, designed for the globally minded African consumer.

From that positioning, everything followed. The colour palette drew from warm earth tones rooted in traditional Nigerian textile traditions. The typography balanced approachability with editorial confidence. The brand mark referenced geometric patterns found in Adire cloth.

## The Work

Deliverables included:

- Full logo system (primary, secondary, icon mark)
- Colour palette and usage guidelines
- Typography selection and pairing rules
- Brand voice guide with writing examples
- Packaging and label design
- Social media template system (9 templates across three formats)
- A 24-page brand guidelines document

## The Outcome

Within three months of the rebrand launch, the business reported a 32% reduction in homepage bounce rate and a 19% increase in average order value — a direct result of the elevated brand perception driving customer confidence at the point of purchase.
